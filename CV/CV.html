About me

I use novel sampling methods, computers and large genetic datasets to answer novel biological questions, typically in marine vertebrates. My research has focused on the development and deployment of a novel marine sampling system capable of simultaneously collecting environmental DNA (eDNA), stereo-video imagery and oceanographic data, investigating the spatial distribution of marine vertebrate species in the context of anthropogenic change; especially the presence of restricted-access marine areas such as Marine Parks and offshore infrastructure. I am highly familiar with the entire eDNA workflow from sampling to analysis, and have successfully implemented this workflow entirely at sea. I also have highly-developed coding skills (full-stack developer), with my recent work focusing on the design and implementation of a custom stack to analyse, store, visualize and interpret eDNA data, including invasive species monitoring. I am passionate about ocean conservation and love working at sea; I sail, windsurf, kitesurf and hydrofoil to a high standard and have previously worked as an internationally certified instructor.
